NASA's SDO Enters Eclipse Season

NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory had witnessed a total eclipse on the 11th of February. This eclipse occured when the Earth passed through its view of the sun. This brief passing marks the start of "eclipse season" for the SDO, it also wasn't too long ago that our own moon also crossed the SDO's view of the sun creating a shadowy image. More info here: https://www.nasa.gov/feature/goddard/2018/eclipse-season-starts-for-nasa-s-sdo

A X8.2-Class Solar Flare is Spotted by the SDO

An intense solar flare was spotted erupting from the sun in the active region of 2673. The designation X8.2 indicates just how intense the flare actually was with X being given to the most intense flares. Although the radiation from the flare won't be able to harm us thanks to our atmosphere if the flare was intense enough it supposedly could interfere with our communication signals. Pretty scary to think that our communications could be disrupted by a solar flare or perhaps cut by a CME!

NASA's GOLD Mission has Launched!

On the 25th of January NASA launched its 'Global-scale Observations of the Limb and Disk' mission, a mission with the goal of giving us a better understanding of space weather. This mission is important to us since it studies the region of space that houses satellites, astronauts at the ISS, GPS signals, and radio signals. I wonder what new information will come about from this new mission?
